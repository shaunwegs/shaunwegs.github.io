Codex of Alchemical Engineering
and the Magnum Opus



These titles are from a fairly legendary indie puzzle game creator, Zachtronics. There's a good chance you aren't familiar with the name. However, you probably know at least one title their work inspired. One of their early games was Inifiniminer, a first-person competitive mining game that takes place in a procedurally generated block world allowing players to mine, build, and explore. That may sound like something you've heard of before... Minecraft, perhaps? and maybe that makes it seem not so impressive... as there are quite a few Minecraft-type games now. Except... Infiniminer was released before Minecraft and was the inspiration for it!

The Codex of Alchemical Engineering was meant to be a more accessible version of Manufactoid, a game about building factories that the founder of Zachtronics had made in college. In Manufactoid, you must build a factory to assemble blocks into different products, with each product being a separate puzzle. It’s most definitely a game for engineers, as it requires a you to both think like an engineer and write Lua code to control your factory.
